  • Fatigue Symptoms

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

      • Prevalence in mild TBI

      • Third most common symptom

      • Manifestations

      • Primary effect (central nervous system-related)
      • Secondary effect (co-existing depression or sleep disturbances)

    • History

      • History

      • Pre/post-injury level of

        Physical activity

        Cognitive function

        Mental health

      • Identify and treat underlying medical and psychological disorders

    • Physical Assessment

      • Physical Assessment

        • Multidimensional Assessment
        of Fatigue (MAF)
      • Fatigue Impact Scale (FIS)
      • Fatigue Assessment Instrument (FAI)

      • Laboratory tests

        Complete blood count (CBC)

        Metabolic panel

        Vitamin B12 and folate

        Thyroid function test

        Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)

    • Medication Review

      If medication appears contributory, perform Applied Behavioral Analysis (ABA) trial to determine the association

    • Referral

    • Patient Education

      Identify factors contributing to fatigue

      Eat well-balanced meals

      Practice sleep hygiene

      Maintain regular exercise

    • Treatment

    • Pharmacologic Treatment

      • Pharmacologic Treatment

      • Address modifiable factors prior to initiating pharmacotherapy

      • Persistent symptoms (more than four weeks) without improvement with sleep, pain, depression and lifestyle, may consider neurostimulant (contraindicated if history of substance abuse):

        Methylphenidate 5mg Q 0800 and Q 1300. Increase total daily dose by 5mg Q 2 weeks to maximum dose of 20mg BID

        Modafanil 100mg QAM. Increase by 100mg, using split daily dosing up to maximum of 400mg/day

      • Amantadine 100–400mg QD

      • Medication trial for at least three months

    • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Well balanced meals
      • Sleep hygiene
      • Regular exercise
      • Cognitive behavioral therapy and physical therapy to improve functional performance

  • Sleep Symptoms

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

      • Prevalence in mild TBI

      • Can occur acutely after injury

      • Manifestations

      • Circadian rhythm sleep disorders
      • Delayed sleep pattern syndrome
      • Irregular sleep-wake pattern

    • History

      • History

      • Evaluate for:

        Sleep routine

        Sleep activity

        Nightmares and frightened arousal

        Other current health conditions that might contribute (i.e., chronic pain)

        Potential cormorbid psychiatric conditions including depression and anxiety

    • Physical Assessment

      • Physical Assessment

      • Examine neck size, airway, height, weight
        • Administer Epworth
        Sleepiness Scale (ESS)         Pittsburgh Sleep
        Quality Index (PSQI)

    • Medication Review

      Medication and supplement use

    • Referral

    • Sleep Study Referral

      • Sleep Study Referral

      • Apnea
      • ESS Greater Than 12
      • Body Mass Index (BMI) Greater Than 30

    • Patient Education

      • Patient Education

      • No caffeine, heavy exercise, alcohol, nicotine, power drinks, stimulants or heavy meals three hours prior to bedtime
      • Avoid bright light exposure near bedtime
      • Keep regular bedtime and wakeup hours
      • Foster quiet, pleasant sleep environment
      • Stop work or TV viewing at least one hour before bedtime
      • Use bed only for sleep and sex
      • Reduce or eliminate daytime naps
      • Consult with healthcare professional before taking any OTC medications or supplements
      • Restrict nighttime sleep period to eight hours
      • Engage in daytime physical and mental activities (within limits of individual’s capacity)
      • Go to another room if sleep does not come within 20–30 minutes
      • Have a relaxing bedtime routine

    • Treatment

    • Pharmacologic Treatment

      • Pharmacologic Treatment

      • Zolpidem 5mg QHS. If poor results after three nights, may increase to 10mg QHS
      • Zolpidem 5–10mg QHS max duration 10 days
      • Trazodone 25–50mg QHS max dose 150mg (sleep maintenance)
      • Amitriptyline 25mg QHS max dose 100mg (headache benefit)

    • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Sleep hygiene
      • Cognitive behavioral therapy focused on sleep with additional behavioral interventions to include sleep restriction, stimulus control, and relapse prevention techniques
      • Reduce stimulation before bedtime

  • Vision Symptoms

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

      • Prevalence in mild TBI

      • 50% (approximately)

      • Manifestations

      • Sensitivity to light, diplopia and blurring

    • Assessments

    • History

      Pre-injury visual deficits

    • Physical Assessment

      • Ophthalmologic examination

      • Extraocular movements
      • Pupils
      • Visual fields by confrontation
      • Fundoscopic exam
      • Visual acuity

    • Referral

    • Optometry (request ocular testing) and Ophthalmology

    • Neurology

      Papilledema

      Cranial nerve deficit

    • Treatment

    • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Initial use of sunglasses followed by formal weaning program (decrease by 15 minutes every two hours)
      • Control environmental light
      • Intermittent patching for double vision
      • Provide reassurance
      • Manage pain

  • Irritability Symptoms

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

      • Manifestations

      • Anger
      • Depression
      • Mood swings
      • Anxiety
      • Tension
      • Easily overwhelmed

    • Assessments

    • History

      • History

        Evaluate specific history and symptoms:

      • Physical fighting
      • Alcohol intake
      • Relationship problems
      • Suicidal
      • Homicidal

    • Physical Assessment

      • Physical Assessment

        • Administer PTSD Checklist
        Military Version (PCL-M)
        screening questionnaire         Consider Patient Health
        Questionnaire (PHQ-9)
        or other depression inventory

    • Referral

    • Psychiatry, psychology and social work

      Outward violence

      Excessive alcohol intake

      Suicidal ideation

      Homicidal ideation

    • Treatment

    • Pharmacologic Treatment

      • Pharmacologic Treatment

      • Sertraline 25–50mg daily. Titrate Q7–10 days. Max dose 150mg/day
      • Citalopram 10mg /day. Titrate to max dose 40mg/day
      • Allow three to four week therapeutic trial of each drug
      • Refer to psychiatry, psychology or social work for treatment failure of two medications

    • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Understand that it is normal to have feelings of anxiety, depression, agitation and feeling overwhelmed
      • Replace negative thoughts and actions with positive ones
      • Refrain from negative self talk (putting oneself down)
      • Talk to someone you love and trust about these concerns
      • Seek emergency care for thoughts or feelings of hurting self or others
      • Seek psychological support if these feelings are causing problems at work or home

  • Nausea Symptoms

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

    • Prevalence and Manifestations

      • Prevalence in mild TBI

      • Occasionally after acute injury

      • Manifestations

      • Usually seen in combination with dizziness (as secondary effect of medications or due to exacerbation of gastroesophageal reflux disease/gastrointestinal (GERD/GI) dysfunction

    • Assessments

    • History

      Define triggers and patterns of nausea

    • Physical Assessment

      • Physical Assessment

      • Perform oropharyngeal examination

    • Medication Review

      Assess medication list for agents that may cause or worsen GI symptoms

    • Treatment

    • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Non-pharmacologic Treatment

      • Reassurance and monitoring
      • Encourage rapid dizziness if contributing to cause of nausea
